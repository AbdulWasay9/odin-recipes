Seekh Kabab

A Kabab that will leave you licking your fingers.

Seekh Kabab is a dish that is charcoaled on a live grill, but exceptions can be made.

Ingredients

  • 3000gm of any meat of your choice.
  • Onions
  • Red Chili
  • Green Chili

Equipment

  1. Kebab Skewers
  2. Chopsticks
  3. Straws

Instructions

  1. Add the Meat, onion, cilantro, mint, and green chili peppers to a food processor. Using the pulse function, finely chop (but not blend) them to a coarse mixture
  2. Add the salt, garlic, ginger, black pepper, red chili flakes, in to the minced meat.
  3. Using gloved hands (do not use bare hands or the green chili may sting), knead for 3-4 minutes, until you begin to see a lacy, stringy texture
  4. Cover the bowl and set aside for 30 minutes, or refrigerate up to overnight.
  5. Using oiled hands, take around 1/4 cup of themeat and form into a hearty round shape. Run the kebab skewers through the meat and use your hands to form a sausage-like shape around the skewer. It should come to 5-6 inches long.
  6. Heat a large, nonstick or cast iron skillet over medium or medium-high heat. Add enough oil to coat the bottom of the pan. When the oil is hot, place the kebabs on the pan, making sure not to crowd them. Use tongs to turn the kebabs frequently, making sure all sides are browned evenly. Cook for 6-7 minutes in total. If you have a meat thermometer, it should read at least 160 Â°F. Transfer the kebabs to a large plate lined with a paper towel.

The Seekh Kabab are ready!
